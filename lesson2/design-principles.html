Design Principles

Rita Bartholomew

Visual Hierarchy

Johnny's Selected Seeds
www.johnnyseeds.com


The website for Johnny's Selected Seeds demonstrates visual hierarchy. The large image with a large block of text immediately draws the viewer’s eye to the center of the page.

From there, the viewer is pulled toward the red rectangle with the word shop.

The red at the top of the page brings the viewers eye up to the shopping card, the hamburger menu, and finally the free shipping.

At that point, the viewer is pulled back to the center and started all over again. The important items are viewed in an easy flowing manner.

White Space and Clean Design

Perennial Solutions
www.perennialsolutions.org


Eric Toensmeier’s website is clean and simple. White space surrounds the website’s welcome and title while acting as a buffer to the two photos.

The hamburger menu appears near the top at the end of a single black bar. The three icons at the bottom are surrounded by white which makes it easy to find them.

The large orange font in the middle blends well with the black, white, and green colors found in the photos which creates a restful, simple, and unhurried feel.

Repetition

Michaels
www.michaels.com


The website for Michaels uses repetition in several ways. The colors red, black, and gray are repeated multiple times and immediately stand out.

In addition to the colors, the use of rectangles both in colors and in white space is noticeable.

Finally, horizontal lines are used multiple times to separate both content and white space.
